The CUD Scale

The CUD scale is a rubric for rating beer and sharing your subjective evaluations with friends. The rating for each beer consists of three factors:

  • Craftsmanship
  • Uniqueness
  • Drinkability

Craftsmanship is rated out of 4:

  1. The beer lacks intention or followthrough.
  2. The beer missed the mark.
  3. The beer does what is intended.
  4. This beer transcends its category.

Uniqueness is rated from 0 to 2:

  • 0 = This beer is not unique.
  • 1 = This beer stands out.
  • 2 = I will not forget this beer.

Drinkability is rated out of 4:

  1. I had some, and I don't need any more.
  2. I drank it and I'm content.
  3. I drank it and I would have it again.
  4. I would have this beer again and again.

The CUD scale was invented in Montana and is an allusion to cud, or the food Montana cows bring from their first stomachs into their mouths to chew again.
